The morning came slowly over the quiet hills, and a thin gray light spread across
the fields. A traveler walked along the narrow road, carrying a small bag and a
walking stick. He had been on the road for many days, and he was glad to see the
distant roofs of the village ahead.

In the village there lived an old clockmaker who knew the name of every star. Each
evening he sat by his window and watched the sky turn from blue to gold to black.
Children would gather at his door to hear him speak of distant lands, of rivers
wider than any field, and of mountains that touched the clouds.

"Time," he told them, "is like a river. It does not stop for anyone, and it never
flows backward. But if you are patient, it will carry you to wonderful places."

The traveler reached the village at noon. He bought bread and cheese from the baker,
drank cool water from the well, and rested beneath a wide and ancient tree. Birds
sang above him, and a gentle wind moved through the leaves. For the first time in
many days, he felt that he was truly home.

That night the stars came out one by one, bright and clear and countless. The old
clockmaker smiled and pointed toward the east, where a single star shone brighter
than the rest. "Follow that one," he said, "and you will never lose your way."
